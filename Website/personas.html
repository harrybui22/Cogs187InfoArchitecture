Information Architecture by Vicky Tu, Heewon Ryu, and Harry Bui

This page contains the five personas we have created to represent part of the target audiences who use the Georgia State University website. Each persona represents a different portion of the target audience and are all distinct from one another. While redesigning the site, we kept these user personas in mind to ensure that we were fulfilling their goals and objectives.

  1. Matt Williams
  2. Helen Patel
  3. Julia Johnson
  4. Walter Nelson
  5. Susan Lee

Matt Williams High school student, 17 years old


Matt has just finished taking his SAT’s and ACT’s and is looking for potential colleges he could attend, specifically specialized for biology majors. He has a special interest in science, and biology has been his favorite subject since elementary school. Although he doesn’t feel too confident with the outcome of his SAT scores, he knows he did pretty well on his ACT’s and if anything, his outstanding GPA will stand out among colleges. He also participates in a few extracurricular activities, including the soccer team as well as volunteering at a homeless shelter on weekends in Los Ageles. Although he can come off shy when meeting new people, he loves hanging out with his friends and eating out.

Scenario 1

Matt’s main concern is the college’s reputation for its degree in biology since working at a biotech company is his dream job.

Question

How strong is GSU’s biology program? How does it compare to other colleges?

User Task

Check out the ranking in biology for GSU and check GSU’s website for its biology program

User Steps

  1. Google GSU’s ranking in biology program
  2. Click on first link: http://grad-schools.usnews.rankingsandreviews.com/best-graduate-schools/top-science-schools/georgia-state-university-139940
  3. Go to gsu.edu
  4. Go to Academics on navbar
  5. Click on Undergraduate Degrees and Majors
  6. Click on Biology
  7. Click on Undergraduate Programs on left sidebar

Scenario 2

Matt wants to know if the college offers soccer as an athletic sport.

Question

What kind of athletic sports does GSU offer?

User Task

Go to GSU’s website and look for soccer in its athletics programs

User Steps

  1. Go to gsu.edu
  2. Click on Athletics on top navbar
  3. Go to Sports on top navbar and click on Men’s Soccer

Scenario 3

Matt would love to meet people who share his interests and make new friends that can compare to his friends at home.

Question

How many student organizations does GSU have?

User Task

Check out the kinds of different student organizations and clubs GSU offers

User Steps

  1. Go to gsu.edu
  2. Go to About on top navbar and click on Student Affairs.
  3. Go to Involvement and click on Student Organizations.

Helen Patel Current student, 18 years old


Helen has just finished her second quarter of her first year at GSU. However after taking some classes as an econ major, she is strongly considering changing her major to something more in her interest. Although she liked economics, she only chose econ because she figured there would be more jobs available for an econ major than just a math major but found econ to be terribly boring. The only fun she had in her econ classes was the math part. She is now thinking of a math degree since she has always liked math. She isn’t sure how difficult college math will be but she knows she excelled in her high school math classes, getting a 5 in her AP Calculus exam. She is glad she realized this earlier on than later and wants to look into the different math programs GSU has to offer.

Scenario 1

Helen wants to know more about the math program at GSU and its B.S. degree program.

Question

What kind of math programs does GSU offer?

User Task

Check out their math program and the classes they offer.

User Steps

  1. Go to gsu.edu
  2. Go to Academics on top navbar and click on Degrees and Majors.
  3. Scroll down and click on Mathematics and Statistics.
  4. Click on Undergraduate on the left sidebar.
  5. She can now choose the different specializations GSU offers for mathematical majors.

Scenario 2

Helen wants to know how difficult it is at GSU to change your major.

Question

How do you change your major at GSU?

User Task

Check out how to change your major by using GSU’s website.

User Steps

  1. Go to gsu.edu
  2. Go to Academics on top navbar and click on Student Advising.
  3. Go to Freshmen on top navbar and click on Changing Your Major.
  4. Take the steps questionnaire they offer.

Scenario 3

Now that she has changed her major to mathematics with a specialization, she is curious about the math graduate program that GSU offers.

Question

What does GSU offer for graduate students?

User Task

Check out GSU’s Graduate program for math majors.

User Steps

  1. Go to gsu.edu
  2. Go to Academics on top navbar and click on Graduate Degrees and Majors.
  3. Scroll down and click on Mathematics and Statistics.
  4. Click on Graduate on left sidebar.
  5. On the bottom of the page, click on Graduate Program Offerings.
  6. Read the information of the pdf.

Julia Johnson Parent, 42 years old


Julia’s daughter will be a first year student at Georgia State University next fall, and Julia wants to know how she can manage her daughter's tuition. Since Julia's daughter is the first of her children to attend college, Julia is unsure about the methods available to manage tuition fees. Throughout high school, Julia employed a tutor to help her daughter with her studies. She is worried that there might be a lack of tutoring resources at the university. Additionally, Julia's daughter has been talking about studying abroad, and Julia is concerned for her daughter's safety. She wants to look up the logistics of studying abroad and find out more information about it to ease her fears.

Scenario 1

Julia wants to look up payment methods to manage her daughter's tuition.

Question

What methods are available for a parent to pay their child's tuition?

User Task

Look up the ways that a parent can manage tuition payments

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Hover over the "Students" tab
  3. Click on "Tuition and payments"

Scenario 2

Julia wants to find out if there are any programs that provide academic mentoring for her daughter.

Question

Are there any mentoring programs available at the university?

User Task

Look up the available mentoring resources for studentss

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Hover over the "Students" tab
  3. Click on the "Student success" tab
  4. Hover over the "First-year programs" tab
  5. Click on the "Academic Coaching" tab

Scenario 3

Julia wants to learn more about studying abroad to ensure that it is safe for her daughter.

Question

How can one find out more about studying abroad?

User Task

Look up the concept of studying abroad as offered by the university

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Hover over the "Global engagement" tab
  3. Click on "Study abroad"
  4. Hover over "Information & resources"
  5. Click on "Parents"

Walter Nelson Incoming professor, 32 years old


Walter just acquired his PhD degree in Computer Science with a specialty in Quantum Computing from Stanford. He is now an incoming professor at Georgia State University, who is going to teach Computer Science classes with an emphasis on Quantum Computing. He is very passionate about his job and wants to teach his students as much as he can. He loves conducting research and is interested in having a research lab open to graduate students and perhaps even a few undergraduate students.

Scenario 1

Walter wants to familiarize himself with the learning management tool that is used by the university.

Question

Which learning management tool does the university use to manage classes?

User Task

Look up the learning management tool that is used by both students and professors

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Hover over the "Faculty & staff" tab
  3. Click on "Faculty" under the "Technology" category
  4. Click on "Learn more" under the "Manage classes in Desire2Learn" section

Scenario 2

Walter wants to ensure that there is sufficient classroom equipment for him to conduct his lectures successfully in the Petit Science Center, room 450.

Question

What equipment is available for classroom use in PSC 450?

User Task

Look up the available equipment that the university offers for a specific classroom

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Hover over the "Faculty & staff" tab
  3. Click on "Faculty" under the "Technology" category
  4. Click on "Search online" under the "Classroom equipment" section
  5. Select "Petit Science Center PSC" under "Select a building"
  6. Select "450" under "Select room"

Scenario 3

Walter wants to know what kind of software is available for him to conduct his research.

Question

What software does the university provide for its researchers?

User Task

Look up the available software used for research

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Hover over the "Faculty & staff" tab
  3. Click on "Faculty" under the "Technology" category
  4. Click on "Research Computering"
  5. Click on "Learn more" under the "Specialized software" section

Susan Lee Alumni, 25 years old


Susan is an alumni who graduated from Georgia State University three years ago. She majored in Computer Science and is working as a coder at Hewlett-Packard as a back-end programmer. Susan was able to greatly enjoy her four years at GSU thanks to the Alumni Association License to Learn scholarship that was awarded to her. She was part of the Computer Science Club at GSU and still keeps in close contact with the organization. Every quarter, the club asks various alumni to speak on a panel at seminars aimed at undergraduate students. Susan always volunteers at every opportunity and feels like she is positively contributing to part of the university community. She is interested in helping students achieve their full potential but contributing to a scholarship fund for students.

Scenario 1

Susan has never made a donation before and would like to see how various alumni donations are being used now.

Question

How does the university use alumni donations now?

User Task

Find out more about how donations are used by the university

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Click on the “Giving” tab on the navigation bar
  3. Click on “Gifts at work”
  4. Select from one of the tabs on the dropdown menu

Scenario 2

Susan wants to join the Alumni Association to be more involved with the university.

Question

How does one join the Alumni Association?

User Task

Look up the Alumni Association to join the organization

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Click on the "Alumni” tab on the navigation bar
  3. Click on the "Join" tab on the navigation bar
  4. Click on "Click here" at the bottom of the page
  5. Select the appropriate membership (GSU Alumni Association Membership)
  6. Complete the form

Scenario 3

Susan is ready to make a donation and would like to complete the necessary steps to make a donation to the university’s Alumni Association Scholarship Fund.

Question

How does one make a donation to the Alumni Association Scholarship Fund?

User Task

Look up the steps that need to be taken in order to make a donation

User Steps

  1. Go to www.gsu.edu
  2. Click on the “Giving” tab on the navigation bar
  3. Click on “Makes a gift”
  4. Select the proper amount
  5. Select “Alumni Association Scholarship Fund” under “Designation”
  6. Complete the form on the next page
  7. Click on “Donate now”
